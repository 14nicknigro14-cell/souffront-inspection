A licensed engineer visits your property and performs a thorough on-site evaluation of the building's structural and electrical condition. We assess visible deficiencies, signs of deterioration, and anything that could affect compliance or safety. You don't need to prepare anything — we handle the entire site visit and document everything.

Our reports are signed and sealed by a licensed Florida engineer and include a full account of the building's observed conditions, any deficiencies identified, and the engineer's professional determination. They're formatted specifically to meet Florida's statutory requirements — ready to submit to your county building department without revisions.

Our reports are accepted by county building departments without revision requests. Because we prepare reports specifically to meet each county's submission requirements — and handle the filing ourselves — the process moves efficiently from inspection to approved recertification.

Most inspections are completed within a few days of scheduling. Report preparation typically follows within one to two weeks. City review timelines vary by county, but because our reports are submitted correctly the first time, you avoid the back-and-forth that causes most delays. We'll give you a specific timeline estimate in your Building Action Plan.
