We start by looking at your building's age, height, location, and what county it's in. Every county has slightly different rules. Once we know those details, we can tell you exactly what's required — whether that's an inspection, a report, a permit, or all three. Most people are surprised by how specific the answer is once someone actually looks at their building.

Cost depends on what we find. Before we give you a number, we look at the size of your building, what type of inspection or work is needed, and whether there are any obvious issues we can see upfront. We don't guess — we scope it first. Once we know what's involved, we give you a clear number with no surprises.

We break it into three parts — the inspection itself, the report, and the city's review. The inspection usually happens within days of scheduling. The report typically follows within a week or two. How long the city takes depends on the county, but because we file everything correctly the first time, you avoid the back and forth that slows most people down.
